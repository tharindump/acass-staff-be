2014

B.Sc. MIT students won Unilever Challenge - Lipton Talent Hunt 2014

The B.Sc MIT undergraduates of the Department of Industrial Management from the University of Kelaniya won the recently held Unilever Challenge – Lipton Talent Hunt-2014, an initiative launched by Unilever Sri Lanka towards empowering Sri Lankan undergraduates in business practices and sound business ideas generation. The Lipton Talent Hunt saw an enthusiastic participation from some of the country’s leading private and public universities.

With the Unilever Challenge – Lipton Talent Hunt, The competition gave the undergraduates business oriented experiential training and also the opportunity to participate in an internationally recognized atmosphere of business. It has captured the imagination of the young university students in Sri Lanka and has also become a benchmark of talent and experience for young undergraduates. The Lipton team contributed to the success of the students, conducting multiple coaching and mentoring sessions, while preparing them for the planning and strategic direction phases. The grand finale was held on 1st of December 2014 Hotel Galadari - Colombo, with four teams who made it to the finals. The participant universities at the finals were the University of Kelaniya, APIIT, University of Colombo and UCSC. This pave them an opportunity to explore and obtain hands-on experience in brand management as a career option at Unilever, while reviewing the possibility of entering a world class career with Unilever.

B.Sc MIT Kelaniya University Team mentored by Mrs. Gamithri Karunasena, impressed the judges with their innovative application and practical approach. The best 3 participants in the Finale will be given the opportunity to visit the Lipton Brand team in UK and will also gain direct entry into the future leaders league London Program.

The platinum sponsors for the event was MAS Holdings. Let us congratulate the editorial panel, the event organizing committee, and IMMSA for holding a yet another excellent event to uphold the department's vision in becoming a center of excellence in knowledge and innovation.

Retirement after 40 years of dedicated service-Prof. Munasinghe & Prof. Degamboda

The faculty and the students of the DIM honored Prof. Munasignhe and Prof. Degamoda retirement after 40 years of dedicated service to the DIM as well as University of Kelaniya on the 22nd of January 2014.

Professor Lalith Munasinghe

He has served as the Head of the Department of Industrial Management for over fifteen years and has contributed immensely to the present success of the department. Professor Munasinghe who was the architect of many programmes at DIM at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels was also a key person behind the introduction of IT and many other important features into the IM curricula. Prof. Munasinghe has also served as the Acting Dean of the Faculty of Science on several occasions. During his tenure as the head of the Computer Unit from December 1990 till September 2000, he commenced the computer literacy programme for the entire student community at the University of Kelaniya in January 1993 for the first time in Sri Lanka, developed study materials in Sinhala, and took the initiative to popularize IT among non-Science graduates. He was commended by the then Vice Chairman, UGC for his contributions to develop IT education in Sri Lanka. He was instrumental in setting up the ‘Centre for Enterprise Management Information  Services’ in  collaboration with UNIDO and the Ministry of Industrial Development, Sri Lanka and has been a key resource person of  many consultancy activities undertaken by DIM. He also played a key role in initiating an advanced international training programme on small and medium enterprise management development for the participants from six countries. As a key person behind the formulation of the Faculty proposal for the Science and Technology Personnel Development Project through which the Faculty received more than Rs. 200 million for PhDs,   short term training, postgraduate scholarships, buildings, equipment, books etc. He also served as the university Coordinator of the Science and Technology Personnel Development Project from 1997 to 2000 where he setup the science faculty postgraduate library and coordinated all activities of the Faculty of Science. The current building of the department with all modern facilities was built at this time according to his vision of creating an environment conducive for higher education. Professor Munasinghe is an exemplary figure in the academic community who had worked whole heartedly for the growth of his department and the education system at large while doing justice to his role as a loving husband and farther.

Professor Sunanda Degamboda

Professor Sunanda Degamboda, is an eminent person in the field of management.

During this time he contributed to the development of the department, University and the nation at large. Professor Degamboda was a key person behind the introduction of many innovative programmes, both at undergraduate and postgraduate levels, at the department. In addition to serving as the head of the Department of Industrial Management, he has been a resource person in the staff development programme and was a key member of the team that developed the initial corporate plan for the University. At the national level, Prof. Degamboda has contributed in areas such as vocational training reforms and skills development. He was a member of Industry sector committee of the National Development Council of the Ministry of Finance, Planning, Ethnic Affairs and National Integration. During the long career of over forty years, Prof. Degamboda has worked with the University of Kelaniya, IRDC(Canada), ROCHE(Canada), Open University of Sri Lanka, OECD(Japan), UNDP, UNIDO, Postgraduate Institute of Management, ITC(Geneva), the World Bank, Commonwealth of Learning, (Canada), Sri Lankan Airlines, over ten leading private companies, and three NGOs in the areas of teaching and training, research, venture management, marketing management, consulting, and entrepreneur and executive training. He has worked as a visiting professor of the Manchester Metropolitan University, University of Keele, and Sheffield Hallam University of UK.

Prof. Degamboda has authored over thirty research articles and books during his academic career and has been a consultant or a trainer to many renowned local and foreign businesses and governments institutions.

CIMA Mind Challenge 2010

Team "MIT - University of Kelaniya" was placed First Runner-Up at the CIMA Mind Challenge 2010, held on the 8th of August 2010 at the Cinnamon Lakeside Hotel. 

The CIMA Mind Challenge, the annual quiz program organized by the CIMA Students' Society, comprised of two rounds. The first round saw 30 teams vying to be one of the top eight teams who would compete in the second round. After a tough first round of questions, team " MIT – University of Kelaniya " secured a place in the top eight. The second round was intensely competitive, with contestants striving to be the first to answer the questions correctly. At the end of it Team " MIT – University of Kelaniya " emerged as the First Runner-Up.


The team comprised of a graduate and four undergraduates of the Department of Industrial Management -Jeevani Perera, Harshani Halangoda and Gian Jayawardena, Chanaka Karunasena, and Thilini Weerasuriya.
