The Transport Engineering Division is an integral part of the Department of Civil Engineering. The division is comprised of four academic staff members who have obtained post-graduate qualifications in different areas of transportation engineering that include traffic engineering, highway engineering and transport planning. We are a leading academics group of Transport & Highway Engineering in Sri Lanka. The division is conducting lectures, practical and research for civil engineering undergraduate students. The division also offers two postgraduate courses; MEng /PG Diploma in Highway & Traffic Engineering and MSc/PG Diploma in Transportation. The academic staff undertakes the supervision of full-time PhD/MPhil/MSc research students and around 8-10 research students work on different areas of research each year.

An increased Transportation Engineering component was introduced to the BSc Civil Engineering curriculum in 1992 and subsequently revised in 2000 and 2009. Under the new curriculum, specialized transportation engineering courses are offered at semester 5, 7 and 8. The semester 5 subject covers an introduction to transport and traffic engineering theory. The compulsory semester 7 subject covers basics in highway geometry, material and design. The optional semester 8 subjects impart theoretical knowledge of traffic engineering, intersection controls, traffic management, pavement design, Geometric design and road safety, highway construction and maintenance management, transport systems planning & operations with project based assignments. Field work including Traffic impact assessments, safety audits, capacity design, signal design and advanced computer packages such as TransPlan, HDM4 and AutoCad AutoDesk Civil 3D are introduced and practical classes have been designed to provide experience in conducting basic traffic and transport surveys and studies.

In addition to conducting the undergraduate and postgraduate course of studies, the Division of Transportation Engineering has been carrying out a fully-fledged research and consultancy programs. Emphasis has been on applied research and the determination of applications for Sri Lanka. Research is also carried out at different levels of the academic program in order to inculcate the principles and practice of research among the students. The TED also undertakes transport studies, traffic surveys, highway design, design of rigid and flexible pavements, feasibility studies, assessments and highway material testing. An emphasis is made on collaborative studies such as capacity building with private and state sector organizations. The division is also conducting Continuous Professional Development Programs (CPD) in all areas of transport. Transportation Engineering Division has two laboratories; highway engineering laboratory - a fully-fledged testing laboratory with latest equipment and transportation engineering laboratory that is equipped with GIS and other software facilities and traffic survey equipment, and a resource center for transport related literature.

The division also equipped with a Road Safety Laboratory located in the Civil Engineering Research Centre. Road Safety Audits and Training Programs on Road Safety are being conducted through this Laboratory.

Advanced Bitumen Testing & Accelerated Pavement Testing Laboratory is the latest laboratary of this division. There is a number of tests to assess the properties of bituminous materials. Bitumen Laboratory facilitates tests for bituminous materials including Marshall test, Superpave test, Accelerated pavement test, and all the other bitumen related tests. It also has a research unit for postgraduate research students. Short course on Highway Material Testing is also conducted annually to share knowledge with industrial personnel in highway engineering field.
